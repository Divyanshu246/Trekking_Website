Frequently Asked Questions

Most tourists can obtain a visa on arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu or at land borders. You can also apply for an e-visa online before traveling. The cost varies by duration: 15 days ($30), 30 days ($50), or 90 days ($125).

Fitness requirements vary by trek. Easy treks require basic fitness, while challenging treks like Everest Base Camp need good cardiovascular endurance. We recommend starting training 3-4 months before your trek with regular hiking, cardio, and strength exercises.

Bring personal items like hiking boots, clothing layers, and rain gear. Heavy items like sleeping bags, down jackets, and trekking poles can be rented in Kathmandu at a fraction of the cost of buying new gear.

All our guides are wilderness first aid certified and carry satellite communication devices. We maintain conservative itineraries with proper acclimatization days, monitor weather conditions closely, and have evacuation procedures in place.

Our prices typically include accommodation during the trek, meals, permits, guide and porter services, and transportation to/from trailheads. International flights, Nepal visa, personal gear, and tips are not included.

You can trek solo with a guide, which many of our clients prefer for the personalized experience. We also organize group treks and can match you with other solo travelers if you prefer company.

The best seasons are pre-monsoon (March-May) and post-monsoon (October-December). Spring offers rhododendron blooms and clear weather, while autumn provides crystal-clear mountain views and stable weather conditions.

Prevention is key: ascend gradually, stay hydrated, avoid alcohol, and listen to your body. Our itineraries include proper acclimatization days. If symptoms develop, the only cure is descent to lower altitude.
